Information About Our Data

Our data comes from scores and rankings taken from the annual Gallup World Survey. The overall Happiness Score is calculated as a combination the following values: GDP Per Capita, Healthy Life Expectancy, Social Support, Freedom to Make Life Choices, Generosity, and Perception of Governmental Corruption. These variables are used to reflect what has been broadly found in the research literature to be important in explaining national-level differences in life evaluations.

Our data value Descriptions

Overall Happiness: Measured as the culmination of sentiment of several values, all of which play a part in the notion of Happiness.
GDP: Gross Domestic Product, the total value of goods produced and services provided in a country during one year.
Social Support: The provision of assistance of comfort to others, typically to help them cope with biological, psychological, and social stressors.
Life Expectancy: The average period that a person may expect to live.
Freedom: The power or right to act, speak, or think as one wants without hinderance or restraint.
Generosity: The quality of being kind and generous to citizens.
Corruption: The level of dishonesty or fraudulent conduct by those in power.
